I create mystical dream landscapes in veiled, muted colors, evoking the faded look of old color photos and early Technicolor films. I see my images as mystery worlds lurking beyond the façade of the palpable and my personal homage to the forces of nature and the cosmos. I am fascinated with creating a sense of vastness and space, faraway vistas with strange formations half seen in the distance--what could they be? Earlier I created cartoons and illustrations of urban angst in pen and ink, but my attention shifted to the natural world and its mysterious energies, perhaps in part due to my practice of tai chi chuan, and in part a desire for solace in the midst of personal loss. Whatever the source, I feel a doorway Village Voice Ilustration opened to the work I do today. Perhaps it was all foreshadowed when as a child my sixth grade teacher, sympathetic to my art leanings, assigned me to create an image of our solar system in color chalks across the classroom blackboard. Unfortunately no photo of the finished “masterpiece” exists, an ephemeral cosmic image in pigmented dust.
I have been drawing since I could hold a crayon. I studied art at Sarah Lawrence College with sculptor Anthony Padovano, at the School of Visual Arts with illustrator Peter Kuper, and classes with comic book artist Ken Landgraf. I did a little work in cartooning and illustration in the 1990's including a spot illustration in the Village Voice in 1993. I was also published in World War 3 Magazine. In 1994 I was a finalist in The Artist's Magazine All-Media Art Competition in the category of pen and ink for the subway design below My Subway Drawing . Years later, I began creating my Cosmic Visions. I am also a professional violist.
