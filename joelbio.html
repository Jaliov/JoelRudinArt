I create imaginary, mystical landscapes in veiled and muted tones. I seek to evoke a sense of vastness and space, faraway vistas with distant formations half seen in the mist, what could they be-- another planet, another dimension, an alternate reality--who knows?
I work mainly in pastel, combined at times with other media, including oil pastel, graphite, china marker, watercolor, I’m always experimenting.
Perhaps it was all foreshadowed in my youth when my sixth grade teacher assigned me to create an image of our solar system in color chalks across the classroom blackboard, ephemeral cosmic images in pigmented dust, I continue that way today.

I have been drawing since I could hold a crayon. I studied art at Sarah Lawrence College with sculptor Anthony Padovano, at the School of Visual Arts with illustrator Peter Kuper, and classes with comic book artist Ken Landgraf. I did a little work in cartooning and illustration in the 1990's including a spot illustration in New York's The Village Voice in 1993. I was also published in World War 3 Magazine. In 1994 I was a finalist in The Artist's Magazine All-Media Art Competition in the category of pen and ink for this subway design My Subway Drawing . Years later, I returned to creating Cosmic Visions. I am also a professional musician.
